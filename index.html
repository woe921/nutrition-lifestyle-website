PROBLEM

Young people (12–18) are navigating a critical stage where habits form that can last a lifetime. Poor nutrition, skipping meals, high sugar intake, ultra-processed foods contributes to low energy, weight gain, and increased risk of chronic diseases like heart disease and diabetes. The issue isn’t just knowledge; it’s motivation, consistency, and social context. Teens often face limited autonomy over food choices, and mixed messages online. A solution that helps make healthy eating feel achievable, rewarding, and socially supported is urgently needed to help this world wide problem.

GOAL

our goal is to help young teenagers from(12-18) all around the world with poor nutrition habits and how they can improve their eating lifestyle.

    Nutrition Myths vs Facts

    Learn the truth about common nutrition beliefs.

    Myth: Carbohydrates are always unhealthy.

    Fact: Carbohydrates provide energy for the body. Whole grains, fruits, and vegetables are healthy sources.

    Myth: Skipping meals helps you stay healthy.

    Fact: Regular meals help maintain energy levels and support balanced nutrition.

    Myth: All fats are bad for you.

    Fact: Healthy fats from foods like nuts, fish, and avocados support brain and heart health.

    Myth: Only protein helps build strength.

    Fact: A balanced diet with carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, and minerals supports overall health.
